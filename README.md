# Heat-Waves
Python workflows and notebooks for detecting, analyzing, and visualizing heatwave events 
## Heat Wave Analysis: Heat waves are prolonged periods of excessively high temperatures that pose severe risks to ecosystems, human health, and
infrastructure. Globally, these events are becoming more frequent, intense, and longer-lasting due to climate change. Rising greenhouse gas emissions have amplified global warming, shifted temperature distributions and increased the likelihood of extreme heat events. Scientific evidence shows that even a small increase in average global temperature significantly raises the probability of record-breaking heat waves. These changes threaten food security, water resources, energy systems, and urban environments, making heat wave analysis critical for climate adaptation, disaster risk reduction, and public health planning
- **P90 Map: 90th percentile (P90) temperature baseline established over a 15-year reference period (2000–2014) to define heatwave thresholds. This percentile-based approach identifies months where temperatures deviate significantly from historical norms, enabling consistent classification of extreme temperature events across the study area. Any monthly mean temperature exceeding this threshold is classified as a heatwave month**  
  ![Heatwave P90](Europe_p90.png)

- **Frequency Map: Number of months globally where the mean temperature exceeded the P90 baseline.​**  
  ![Heatwave Frequency](Europe_frequency.png)

- **Intensity Map:  Cumulative sum of temperature exceedances (in °C), providing a measure of warming magnitude beyond the climatological norm.​**  
  ![Heatwave Intensity](Europe_intensity.png)
